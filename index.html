Justin Lidard

Welcome! I’m a PhD candidate at Princeton, where I work with Anirudha Majumdar and Naomi Leonard on robotics. During my PhD I’ve spent time at Toyota Research Institute working on human-interactive driving.

I design algorithms that enable robots to intelligently interact with and learn from people. On of my key interests is value alignment from an optimal control perspective: AI systems may offer strong performance but lack nuance in their understanding of intuitive and safe behaviors. To bridge this gap, I am working to enable robots closely mimic people through imitation while still retaining the ability to optimize their long-term behavior. My long-term goal is to enable robots to continuously adjust their strategies through feedback during training, evaluation, and deployment.
